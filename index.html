Project Goal:¶

Our Goal is to analyse TSA data to see if the speed or efficency of TSA has changed over time. By analyzing metrics such as total passengers traveling through TSA and speed in which TSA processes said passengers, in comparison with upgrades to TSA systems and global security events, we hope to analyze the relationship between increases to security and the time it takes to pass through TSA checkpoints at airports across America.

Table 1:¶

Our first dataset comes from https://data.bts.gov/Aviation/Commercial-Aviation-Departures-and-TSA-Screenings-/hvq3-38u5/about_data. This data set shows the amount of passengers processed by TSA on different dates, for both domestic and international flights starting in 2019, with the most recent update in 2023. The question we hope to answer with this data is how did the amount of both domestic and international passengers coming through TSA everyday change overtime. There are 6 variables, and 4115 observations. The variables are mode of transportation (all are aviation), indicator (a variable indicating what "current" represents, either domestic, international, or both), date, "current" (a number indicating the amount of people processed through TSA in that week), the week number, and the year. Interestingly, a majority of the dates covered repeat three times, one for each value of indicator.
